Brisbane Floods - A Timeline

About

This project is designed to present articles and photos of Brisbane's flood history in an engaging and user-friendly way. Photos and newspaper articles are sourced from the National Library of Australia, a massive collection of Australian resources. You can view information on major flood events, or apply specific filters to find what you're looking for.


Usage

To browse by major flood event, click one of the buttons at the top of the page. You can toggle between these timeline buttons and advanced search filters by clicking a link on the right. You can browse articles, and view the original print. You can also view photos, tagged by location on the map, by clicking a map marker. This will show a brief overview with a link to the Trove record. Clicking a photo thumbnail will display the full size image.

Background

This web application was created by students as part of a project at the University of Queensland, and uses the National Library of Australia's Trove API. The goal was to somehow utilise Trove records in an interesting and useful way.


Notes

By default, 30 articles and a maximum of 100 photos are displayed for each search. The maximum number of records which can be returned by a single search is 100. To load more, click 'See more' at the bottom of the Articles list, or 'Load more photos' below the map.

If no articles are returned, your search is probably too recent. Trove only contains records of articles from before 2000 (as of writing).

This application will work best in a modern browser and probably won't display correctly on a mobile browser.
